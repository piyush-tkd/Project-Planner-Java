🏆 Where Portfolio Planner Wins

1. Broadest single-platform coverage. No other tool in this landscape covers portfolio management + resource capacity + Jira analytics + engineering intelligence + AI querying + scenario simulation in one product. The closest requires 3-4 tools combined.

2. Deepest Jira integration of any PMO tool. 20+ synced entities, worklog analysis, support queue SLA, sprint analytics, and a custom dashboard builder. Tempo comes close but lacks portfolio-level intelligence. Jellyfish has analytics but no project management.

3. AI-native architecture. The NLP assistant with pgvector semantic search, self-learning patterns, and streaming responses is more sophisticated than anything in Jira, Smartsheet, or Wrike. Only Jellyfish's AI assistant is comparable — but it lacks the PM/resource context.

4. Scenario simulation without data contamination. Named scenarios with isolated overrides for what-if analysis. Planview has this, but at $45+/user/mo. None of the mid-market tools (Monday, Asana, Wrike) offer it.

5. Financial intelligence purpose-built for engineering. CapEx vs OpEx at the Jira issue level, ROI per POD, budget variance — this is Jellyfish + Tempo territory, unified with portfolio context.

Position: Portfolio Planner sits in a unique quadrant — high on both PPM and engineering analytics axes. It's the only platform that doesn't force you to choose between portfolio governance (Planview) and engineering intelligence (Jellyfish).

vs. Jira + Tempo + Jellyfish stack (~$80/user/mo combined) — You offer comparable capability at potentially lower TCO in a single pane of glass. Trade-off: Jira is the execution engine they already own.
